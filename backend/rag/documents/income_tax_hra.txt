INCOME TAX BENEFITS ON HOME LOANS AND HRA - COMPLETE GUIDE
============================================================

1. SECTION 80C - PRINCIPAL REPAYMENT DEDUCTION
- Maximum deduction: ₹1,50,000 per financial year
- Covers: Principal portion of home loan EMI
- Also covers: Stamp duty and registration charges (in year of payment)
- Condition: Property should not be sold within 5 years of possession
- If sold within 5 years: Deduction previously claimed gets added back to income

2. SECTION 24(b) - INTEREST ON HOME LOAN
For Self-Occupied Property:
- Maximum deduction: ₹2,00,000 per financial year
- Condition: Construction must be completed within 5 years from end of FY in which loan was taken
- If construction not completed in 5 years: Maximum deduction reduces to ₹30,000
- Pre-construction interest: Can be claimed in 5 equal annual installments starting from year of completion

For Let-Out / Rented Property:
- No upper limit on interest deduction
- Entire interest amount can be claimed against rental income
- If rental income minus interest results in loss: Can set off up to ₹2,00,000 against other income
- Remaining loss can be carried forward for 8 assessment years

3. SECTION 80EE - ADDITIONAL INTEREST DEDUCTION (FIRST-TIME BUYERS)
- Additional deduction: ₹50,000 per financial year
- Condition: Loan sanctioned between April 2016 - March 2017
- Loan amount should not exceed ₹35 lakh
- Property value should not exceed ₹50 lakh
- Borrower should not own any other house on date of loan sanction
- Note: This section has limited applicability now

4. SECTION 80EEA - AFFORDABLE HOUSING INTEREST DEDUCTION
- Additional deduction: ₹1,50,000 per financial year
- Condition: Loan sanctioned between April 2019 - March 2022
- Stamp value of property should not exceed ₹45 lakh
- Borrower should not own any other residential property on date of loan sanction
- First-time homebuyer only
- This is OVER AND ABOVE the Section 24(b) limit
- Total interest deduction possible: ₹2,00,000 + ₹1,50,000 = ₹3,50,000

5. HRA (HOUSE RENT ALLOWANCE) EXEMPTION
Calculation of HRA Exemption (least of the following three):
a) Actual HRA received from employer
b) Rent paid minus 10% of basic salary
c) 50% of basic salary (metro cities) or 40% of basic salary (non-metro)

HRA Exemption NOT available if:
- You live in your own house
- You don't pay any rent
- You receive HRA but don't have rental receipts

HRA and Home Loan Tax Benefits Together:
- YES, both can be claimed simultaneously
- Scenario 1: You have a home loan on an under-construction property and pay rent elsewhere
- Scenario 2: You own a house in one city but work and rent in another city
- Scenario 3: You have rented out your owned property and live in a rented house
- Documentation: Keep rental agreement, rent receipts, and landlord PAN (if rent > ₹1 lakh/year)

6. TAX BENEFIT CALCULATION EXAMPLE

Assume:
- Annual salary: ₹12,00,000
- Basic salary: ₹6,00,000
- HRA received: ₹3,00,000
- Rent paid: ₹2,40,000 (₹20,000/month)
- Home loan principal repaid: ₹2,00,000
- Home loan interest paid: ₹3,50,000
- City: Mumbai (metro)

HRA Exemption (least of):
a) Actual HRA = ₹3,00,000
b) Rent - 10% of basic = ₹2,40,000 - ₹60,000 = ₹1,80,000
c) 50% of basic = ₹3,00,000
→ HRA exemption = ₹1,80,000

Section 80C deduction = ₹1,50,000 (capped, though ₹2,00,000 paid)
Section 24(b) deduction = ₹2,00,000 (capped, though ₹3,50,000 paid)

Total tax savings:
- HRA: ₹1,80,000 × 30% tax rate = ₹54,000
- 80C: ₹1,50,000 × 30% = ₹45,000
- 24(b): ₹2,00,000 × 30% = ₹60,000
- Total annual tax saving: ~₹1,59,000

7. JOINT HOME LOAN TAX BENEFITS
- Each co-borrower can claim deductions independently
- Each can claim up to ₹1.5 lakh under 80C
- Each can claim up to ₹2 lakh under 24(b)
- Condition: Both must be co-owners of the property
- Combined benefit for couple: Up to ₹3 lakh (80C) + ₹4 lakh (24b) = ₹7 lakh deductions

8. NEW TAX REGIME vs OLD TAX REGIME
Old Regime:
- All home loan deductions available (80C, 24b, 80EEA)
- HRA exemption available
- Higher tax rates but more deductions

New Regime (Section 115BAC):
- Section 80C NOT available
- Section 24(b) for self-occupied NOT available
- Section 24(b) for rented property: Available (set off up to ₹2 lakh)
- HRA exemption NOT available
- Lower tax rates but fewer deductions
- Recommended for people with limited deductions/investments

9. DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST
- Loan certificate from bank (showing principal and interest breakup)
- Property possession certificate
- Completion certificate (from builder/authority)
- Rent receipts (for HRA claim)
- Rental agreement
- Form 16 from employer
- PAN of landlord (if rent exceeds ₹1,00,000/year)
- Property registration documents

10. TIPS FOR MAXIMIZING TAX BENEFITS
- Consider joint home loan with spouse to double deductions
- Time your property possession to start claiming benefits early
- Keep track of pre-construction interest for future claims
- Choose between old and new tax regimes strategically
- Maintain all documentation for at least 7 years after filing
- Claim stamp duty and registration under 80C in the year of purchase

RBI MASTER CIRCULAR ON HOUSING FINANCE (Summary)
=================================================

Reference: RBI/2024-25/Master Circular on Housing Finance
Date: Updated April 2024

1. INTRODUCTION
Housing finance is a critical component of India's banking and financial system. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has issued comprehensive guidelines for housing loan operations by banks and housing finance companies (HFCs).

2. LOAN-TO-VALUE (LTV) RATIO
- For loans up to ₹30 lakh: Maximum LTV is 90%
- For loans between ₹30 lakh and ₹75 lakh: Maximum LTV is 80%
- For loans above ₹75 lakh: Maximum LTV is 75%
- The LTV ratio determines the minimum down payment required from the borrower.

3. INTEREST RATE GUIDELINES
- Banks may offer fixed or floating rate home loans
- Floating rates are linked to external benchmarks (EBLR/RLLR)
- Current repo rate: 6.50% (as of 2024)
- Typical spread: 2.0% to 3.5% over repo rate
- Effective home loan rates range from 8.35% to 11.50% depending on borrower profile

4. RISK WEIGHTS
- Loans up to ₹30 lakh with LTV ≤ 80%: Risk weight 35%
- Loans between ₹30-75 lakh with LTV ≤ 80%: Risk weight 35%
- Loans above ₹75 lakh: Risk weight 50%
- Higher LTV ratios attract higher risk weights

5. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
- Age: 21-65 years (at maturity)
- Minimum employment: 2 years for salaried, 3 years for self-employed
- Credit Score: Minimum 650 preferred; 750+ for best rates
- Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio: Should not exceed 50% of net monthly income
- FOIR (Fixed Obligations to Income Ratio): Maximum 50-60%

6. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
For Salaried Borrowers:
- Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)
- Address proof
- Income proof: Last 3 months salary slips + Form 16
- Bank statements: Last 6 months
- Property documents
- Employment proof: Appointment letter / Experience certificate

For Self-Employed Borrowers:
- Identity and address proof
- Business proof: GST registration, trade license
- ITR for last 3 years
- Audited financial statements
- Bank statements: Last 12 months
- Property documents

7. NPA CLASSIFICATION
- Standard Asset: Less than 90 days overdue
- Sub-Standard Asset: 90 days to 12 months overdue
- Doubtful Asset: More than 12 months overdue
- Loss Asset: Identified as uncollectible

8. PREPAYMENT AND FORECLOSURE
- No prepayment penalty on floating rate home loans (RBI circular)
- Banks cannot levy foreclosure charges on floating rate loans
- Fixed rate loans may have prepayment charges as per bank policy

9. TAX BENEFITS
- Section 80C: Deduction on principal repayment up to ₹1.5 lakh per annum
- Section 24(b): Deduction on interest payment up to ₹2 lakh per annum (self-occupied)
- Section 80EEA: Additional deduction of ₹1.5 lakh for first-time homebuyers (stamp value ≤ ₹45 lakh)

10. COMPLAINT AND GRIEVANCE
- Borrowers can file complaints with RBI Ombudsman
- Banks must have internal grievance redressal mechanism
- RERA registration mandatory for under-construction properties

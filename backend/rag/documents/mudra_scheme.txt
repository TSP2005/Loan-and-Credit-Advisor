MUDRA LOAN SCHEME (PRADHAN MANTRI MUDRA YOJANA - PMMY)
=======================================================

1. OVERVIEW
Launched on April 8, 2015, PMMY provides loans up to ₹10 lakh to non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises. The scheme is available through banks, MFIs, and NBFCs under the MUDRA framework.

2. THREE CATEGORIES OF MUDRA LOANS

SHISHU (शिशु):
- Loan amount: Up to ₹50,000
- Target: Startups and new businesses
- Interest rate: ~10-12% per annum
- No collateral required
- Processing fee: Nil to minimal
- Repayment: Up to 5 years
- Best for: Small vendors, artisans, hawkers starting new ventures

KISHORE (किशोर):
- Loan amount: ₹50,001 to ₹5,00,000
- Target: Growing businesses seeking to expand
- Interest rate: ~10-14% per annum
- No collateral required
- Processing fee: 0.5% of loan amount
- Repayment: Up to 5 years
- Best for: Small shop owners, service providers expanding operations

TARUN (तरुण):
- Loan amount: ₹5,00,001 to ₹10,00,000
- Target: Established enterprises scaling up
- Interest rate: ~12-16% per annum
- Collateral may be required for higher amounts
- Processing fee: 0.5% of loan amount
- Repayment: Up to 7 years
- Best for: Manufacturing units, traders, service enterprises

3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
- Any Indian citizen who has a business plan for a non-farm income generating activity
- Business should be a micro/small enterprise
- Activity should be in manufacturing, trading, or service sector
- Existing businesses or new startups both eligible
- No minimum educational qualification required
- SC/ST/OBC and women entrepreneurs get priority

4. ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES
Manufacturing Sector:
- Food processing and preservation
- Textile and garment manufacturing
- Handicrafts and handloom
- Wood products and furniture
- Paper products and printing
- Chemical and pharmaceutical products
- Electrical and transport equipment
- Repair of vehicles, computers, personal goods

Service Sector:
- Salons, beauty parlors, tailoring
- Dry cleaning and laundry
- Photocopying and documentation
- Coaching and tuition centers
- Small restaurants and food stalls
- Auto repair and servicing
- Transport and logistics

Trading Activities:
- Shops (retail and wholesale)
- Hawkers and street vendors
- E-commerce platforms

5. APPLICATION PROCESS
Step 1: Identify the nearest bank branch (public sector, private, RRB, or MFI)
Step 2: Download MUDRA loan application form (or get from bank)
Step 3: Prepare a project report / business plan
Step 4: Submit application with required documents
Step 5: Bank verifies documents and creditworthiness
Step 6: Loan sanctioned and disbursed (typically within 7-14 days for Shishu)

6. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
- Identity proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, PAN card)
- Address proof (utility bill, rental agreement)
- Business proof (GST registration, shop license, trade license)
- Passport-sized photographs
- Bank statements (last 6 months, if existing account)
- Project report / Business plan
- Quotations for machinery/equipment (if applicable)
- Category certificate (SC/ST/OBC, if applicable)
- ITR (for Kishore and Tarun categories)

7. KEY FEATURES
- No collateral required for loans up to ₹10 lakh (MUDRA card facility)
- MUDRA Card: Works like a credit card for working capital needs
- Credit Guarantee through CGFMU (Credit Guarantee Fund for Micro Units)
- Women borrowers constitute ~68% of total MUDRA loan accounts
- Priority to SC/ST entrepreneurs
- Refinancing through MUDRA (Micro Units Development & Refinance Agency Ltd.)

8. STATISTICS (As of 2024)
- Total loans sanctioned: Over 44 crore accounts since inception
- Total amount disbursed: Over ₹23.5 lakh crore
- Shishu category: 88% of accounts (by number)
- Women borrowers: 68% of total accounts
- SC/ST borrowers: 22% of total accounts

9. REPAYMENT AND DEFAULT
- Monthly EMI-based repayment
- No prepayment penalty
- Default leads to classification as NPA after 90 days
- Recovery through normal banking channels
- Government does not guarantee individual loans
- Borrower's credit score gets affected on default

10. TIPS FOR APPLICANTS
- Prepare a clear business plan showing expected income and expenses
- Maintain a good credit history (if any previous borrowing)
- Start with Shishu if this is your first business loan
- Keep all business-related documents organized
- Explore MUDRA card for working capital flexibility
- Ensure timely repayment to build credit history for future larger loans

PRADHAN MANTRI AWAS YOJANA (PMAY) - COMPREHENSIVE GUIDELINES
=============================================================

1. OVERVIEW
PMAY is a flagship government housing scheme launched in 2015 with the vision of "Housing for All." The scheme provides affordable housing to the urban and rural poor through various components.

2. PMAY-URBAN COMPONENTS

Component 1: In-Situ Slum Redevelopment (ISSR)
- For slum dwellers in urban areas
- Central assistance of ₹1 lakh per house
- States/UTs to provide additional subsidies

Component 2: Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS)
- Interest subsidy on home loans for EWS, LIG, MIG-I, and MIG-II categories
- EWS/LIG (income up to ₹6 lakh): Subsidy of 6.5% on loan up to ₹6 lakh for 20 years
- MIG-I (income ₹6-12 lakh): Subsidy of 4% on loan up to ₹9 lakh for 20 years
- MIG-II (income ₹12-18 lakh): Subsidy of 3% on loan up to ₹12 lakh for 20 years
- Net Present Value (NPV) of subsidy is credited upfront to borrower's loan account

Component 3: Affordable Housing in Partnership (AHP)
- Central assistance of ₹1.5 lakh per EWS house
- Built on public or private land

Component 4: Beneficiary-led Individual House Construction (BLC)
- Central assistance of ₹1.5 lakh for individual house construction/enhancement
- For EWS category in urban areas

3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

Income Categories:
- EWS (Economically Weaker Section): Annual household income up to ₹3 lakh
- LIG (Lower Income Group): Annual household income ₹3-6 lakh
- MIG-I (Middle Income Group I): Annual household income ₹6-12 lakh
- MIG-II (Middle Income Group II): Annual household income ₹12-18 lakh

Basic Eligibility:
- Beneficiary family should not own a pucca house anywhere in India
- For CLSS: At least one female member must be co-owner (EWS/LIG)
- Aadhaar card mandatory for identification
- First-time home buyer preference

Carpet Area Limits:
- EWS: Up to 30 sq.m. (323 sq.ft.)
- LIG: Up to 60 sq.m. (645 sq.ft.)
- MIG-I: Up to 160 sq.m. (1,722 sq.ft.)
- MIG-II: Up to 200 sq.m. (2,153 sq.ft.)

4. SUBSIDY CALCULATION EXAMPLE

For an LIG family taking a ₹20 lakh home loan at 9% for 20 years:
- Without PMAY: EMI = ₹17,995/month
- PMAY subsidy on ₹6 lakh at 6.5% for 20 years = ₹2,67,280 (NPV)
- Effective loan reduces to ₹17,32,720
- New EMI = ₹15,582/month
- Monthly savings = ₹2,413

5. APPLICATION PROCESS
Step 1: Visit the nearest Common Service Centre (CSC) or bank
Step 2: Fill PMAY application form with required documents
Step 3: Submit Aadhaar, income proof, and property documents
Step 4: Verification by Urban Local Body (ULB)
Step 5: Approval and subsidy disbursement through lending institution

6. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
- Aadhaar card (mandatory)
- Income certificate from competent authority
- PAN card
- Address proof
- Self-declaration of not owning a pucca house
- Caste certificate (if applicable for reservation)
- Bank account details

7. PMAY-GRAMIN (Rural)
- Target: 2.95 crore houses in rural areas
- Assistance: ₹1.20 lakh (plain areas), ₹1.30 lakh (hilly/difficult areas)
- Additional ₹12,000 for toilet construction under Swachh Bharat Mission
- 90 days of employment under MGNREGA for skilled/unskilled labor

8. IMPORTANT NOTES
- Scheme extended to December 31, 2024 (PMAY-Urban 2.0 announced)
- No income limit for rental housing under PMAY-Urban 2.0
- Digital identification through Aadhaar-based verification
- Geo-tagging of beneficiary houses for monitoring
- Anti-duplication mechanism to prevent duplicate benefits

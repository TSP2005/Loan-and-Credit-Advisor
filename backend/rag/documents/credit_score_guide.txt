CREDIT SCORE IN INDIA - COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
=============================================

1. WHAT IS A CREDIT SCORE?
A credit score is a three-digit number (typically 300-900 in India) that represents your creditworthiness. It is calculated by credit bureaus based on your credit history, payment behavior, and financial discipline.

2. CREDIT BUREAUS IN INDIA
- CIBIL (TransUnion CIBIL): Most widely used, CIBIL Score
- Experian India: Experian Credit Score
- Equifax India: Equifax Credit Score
- CRIF High Mark: High Mark Score

All four bureaus use slightly different algorithms but the score range is typically 300-900.

3. CREDIT SCORE RANGES AND MEANING

Excellent (750-900):
- Best interest rates available
- Quick loan approval
- Higher loan amounts sanctioned
- Pre-approved offers from banks
- Premium credit card eligibility

Good (700-749):
- Favorable interest rates
- Good approval chances
- Standard loan amounts
- Most credit products accessible

Fair (650-699):
- Moderate interest rates (higher than average)
- Approval possible but may take longer
- Lower loan amounts
- Limited credit card options
- May need additional documentation

Below Average (550-649):
- High interest rates
- Difficult to get approval from major banks
- May need collateral for all loans
- Limited to personal loans from NBFCs
- Credit rebuilding recommended

Poor (300-549):
- Very high interest rates if approved
- Most banks will reject applications
- Only secured loans may be available
- Urgent credit repair needed
- May need co-applicant or guarantor

4. FACTORS AFFECTING CREDIT SCORE

Payment History (35% weight):
- Timely payment of EMIs, credit card bills
- Late payments severely damage score
- Even one 90-day delay can drop score by 50-100 points
- Payment history is the single most important factor

Credit Utilization (30% weight):
- Ratio of used credit to available credit limit
- Ideal utilization: Below 30%
- High utilization (>50%) indicates financial stress
- Keep credit card spending below 30% of limit

Credit History Length (15% weight):
- Longer credit history = better score
- Keep old credit cards active (even with zero balance)
- Average age of all credit accounts matters
- Closing oldest account can hurt score

Credit Mix (10% weight):
- Having different types of credit (home loan, car loan, credit card)
- Mix of secured and unsecured loans
- Shows ability to handle different credit types

New Credit Inquiries (10% weight):
- Multiple loan applications in short time = multiple hard inquiries
- Each hard inquiry can reduce score by 5-10 points
- Soft inquiries (self-check) do not affect score
- Space out credit applications by at least 3-6 months

5. HOW TO IMPROVE CREDIT SCORE

Immediate Steps (0-3 months):
- Pay all outstanding dues immediately
- Set up auto-pay for all EMIs and credit card bills
- Reduce credit card utilization below 30%
- Dispute any errors in credit report

Short-term Steps (3-6 months):
- Use credit card regularly but pay full amount
- Don't close old credit cards
- Avoid applying for new credit
- Become an authorized user on someone's good credit card

Medium-term Steps (6-12 months):
- Consider a secure credit card to rebuild
- Mix credit types (add a small personal loan if only cards)
- Maintain zero defaults for 6+ consecutive months
- Review credit report quarterly for errors

Long-term Steps (12+ months):
- Maintain consistent payment history
- Gradually increase credit limit requests
- Keep total debt manageable
- Plan major credit applications strategically

6. CREDIT SCORE AND LOAN ELIGIBILITY

Home Loan:
- Minimum recommended: 650
- Best rates available at: 750+
- Rate difference: ~0.25-1.0% between 650 and 800 scores

Personal Loan:
- Minimum recommended: 700
- Best rates at: 750+
- Many banks reject below 700

Car Loan:
- Minimum recommended: 650
- Best rates at: 750+
- Lower score may require higher down payment

Credit Card:
- Basic cards: 650+
- Premium/reward cards: 750+
- Super-premium cards: 800+

7. CHECKING YOUR CREDIT SCORE
- One free credit report per year from each bureau (RBI mandate)
- CIBIL: https://www.cibil.com
- Experian: https://www.experian.in
- Equifax: https://www.equifax.co.in
- Third-party apps: Paytm, BankBazaar, CRED (free scores)

8. COMMON MYTHS DEBUNKED
- Myth: Checking your own score lowers it → FALSE (soft inquiry)
- Myth: Closing credit cards improves score → Usually FALSE
- Myth: High salary means high credit score → FALSE (income not a factor)
- Myth: Debit card usage builds credit → FALSE (only credit products)
- Myth: Settling a loan is as good as paying in full → FALSE (settlement is negative)

9. DEBT-TO-INCOME (DTI) RATIO
- Definition: Total monthly debt payments / Gross monthly income × 100
- Ideal DTI: Below 36%
- Acceptable DTI: 36-43%
- Risky DTI: 43-50%
- High Risk DTI: Above 50%
- Banks typically won't lend if total DTI (including new loan) exceeds 50%

10. CREDIT SCORE FOR SPECIFIC POPULATIONS
- New-to-Credit (NTC): Start with secured credit card or small loan
- Students: Consider student credit cards with low limits
- Senior Citizens: Maintain existing accounts, avoid new debt
- Self-Employed: Maintain separate business and personal credit
